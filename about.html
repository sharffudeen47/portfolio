The Person Behind the Portfolio

I like building things that last, then making them harder to break. This page is a quick look at how I think, what I care about, and the kind of work I want to do more of.

My Journey

I’m Mohamed Sharfuddeen Nadeem, based in Bruges. I studied Applied Computer Science at Howest, where I spent a lot of time understanding how networks and systems behave in the real world. Over time, security became less of a topic and more of a way I approach everything: learn the fundamentals, test the assumptions, and improve what matters.

My Focus

I focus on the internal side of security and infrastructure. That means visibility, validation, and hardening: mapping what exists, confirming what is risky, and turning those findings into practical steps. I also like automation with Python and Bash, not to replace thinking, but to make repeatable work consistent and reliable.

My Goal

I’m looking for roles where engineering and security work together. Junior Security Engineer, SOC or Blue Team, or Security Consultant roles all interest me, as long as the environment values doing the work properly and communicating it clearly to the people who need to act on it.

Hands-On Defense

During my internship at WZC De Foyer, I worked on real networks in a healthcare environment where availability matters. I identified exposed services, validated risks, and helped shape hardening actions that made sense operationally, especially for devices that do not fit standard security playbooks.

Versatile Toolkit

I work comfortably across Linux and Windows environments. I use tools like Nmap and Wireshark to learn what is happening, then dig into why it is happening. I care about the details because small configuration choices often create the biggest security gaps.

Clarity Matters

A finding is only useful if people understand it and can act on it. I put effort into clean documentation and structured reporting so that technical teams can implement fixes and non-technical stakeholders can understand priorities, risk, and trade-offs.
