Hack The Future Antwerpen 2025: What Pressure Teaches You

My short reflection on teamwork, problem solving, and learning fast during Hack The Future on 12 November 2025.

On 12 November 2025, I joined Hack The Future Antwerpen 2025, a hackathon organized by De Cronos Groep. It brought students together for challenges that feel close to real work. You have limited time, you work with new people, and you have to make decisions quickly.

The biggest challenge was not just the technical part. It was staying in sync as a team. We had to divide tasks, communicate fast, and still keep the quality good. We reached a 62% score and finished 3rd out of 12 teams, and I’m honestly proud of that. It came from keeping calm, testing ideas quickly, and trusting each other.

The challenges covered topics like AI, cloud, and automation. One thing I noticed is that structure helps more than people think. Even simple things like a checklist, clear notes, and quick validation can save a lot of time. That feels very similar to cybersecurity work, where you should prove what you claim and work with evidence.

This event also connected well with what I learned at Arxus. There, I worked with Terraform and Microsoft Azure. Infrastructure as code teaches you a simple lesson: if you cannot rebuild it, you do not fully control it. That idea fits security too. Repeatable setups and clear documentation make things safer.

A personal thank you to Tibo Eycken for making the day more fun and motivating, and to Arxus for the support and the real challenges that helped me grow. Hack The Future reminded me why I like this field. It rewards curiosity, teamwork, and learning under pressure.
