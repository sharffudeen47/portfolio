.NET 10 Demystified: Real Demos, Good Energy, and Solid Engineering

A short reflection on the Howest Tech and Meet evening with Kevin De Rudder. Performance updates, C# changes, Aspire and Blazor demos, and a good reminder that clean engineering helps security too.

I attended the Howest Tech and Meet session on “.NET 10 Demystified” by Kevin De Rudder. It was a really enjoyable evening with a lot of real demos and a relaxed atmosphere.

I usually go to cybersecurity and AI talks, so this one felt like a nice change. It had strong developer energy: live coding, practical examples, and small jokes that kept the room awake. It felt natural and easy to follow.

Speaker
Kevin De Rudder
Topic
.NET 10 Demystified
Style
Hands-on and practical
What I liked most

It did not feel like a long list of features. It felt like advice from someone who actually builds things and knows what helps in real projects.

Highlights from the session

  • Live demos: less theory, more real examples.
  • Performance focus: improvements that make apps feel smoother and more stable.
  • C# updates: small changes that make code cleaner and easier to read.
  • Aspire and Blazor: good demos that showed how the ecosystem fits together.
  • Good vibe: fun moments, good crowd, and easy networking.

Why it mattered to me

From a security point of view, this was a good reminder: many security problems start with messy software. When code is easier to maintain and systems behave in a predictable way, it becomes easier to operate and secure them.

My simple security takeaway

Better defaults and cleaner patterns reduce mistakes. Fewer shortcuts usually means fewer hidden problems later.

What I’m keeping from it

  • Performance is also stability. Stable systems are easier to run and troubleshoot.
  • The ecosystem feels connected. You can build real apps with less friction.
  • Clean code scales better. It stays understandable longer.
  • Learning with others helps. Hearing how experienced developers think is valuable.

Overall, it was a great session. Thanks to Kevin and Howest for organizing it and keeping it practical and fun.
